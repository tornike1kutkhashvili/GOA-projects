BMW history

how BMW established?

BMW (Bayerische Motoren Werke AG) was founded in 1916 in Munich, Germany. The name of the company means “Bavarian Motor Works”. At the beginning, BMW did not produce cars — its main focus was the production of aircraft engines. After the end of World War I, Germany was forbidden to manufacture aircraft engines. Because of this restriction, BMW changed its direction and began producing motorcycles and industrial engines. In 1923, the company released its first motorcycle, the BMW R32, which became very successful. BMW entered automobile production in 1928. The first car produced by the company was the BMW Dixi, a licensed version of the British Austin 7. From this moment, BMW started to develop as a car manufacturer. The BMW logo, often associated with a spinning airplane propeller, actually represents the blue and white colors of the Bavarian flag, showing the company’s origins. Today, BMW is one of the world’s leading premium automobile manufacturers, known for its innovation, sporty character, and high quality. The brand’s slogan — “The Ultimate Driving Machine” — perfectly reflects its philosophy.

BMW's first car, the Dixi, was launched in 1928. It was a licensed version of the British Austin 7 and marked BMW's entry into automobile manufacturing. The Dixi was a small, reliable car designed for everyday use, and it helped establish BMW as a respected car manufacturer.

BMW's First Major Success: The 328 Roadster

BMW achieved its first major success in the automobile industry with the BMW 328 Roadster, introduced in 1936. This sports car was celebrated for its advanced engineering, lightweight design, and impressive performance. The 328 quickly gained a reputation in both racing and road driving, winning numerous competitions, including the famous Monte Carlo Rally.

The BMW 328 established the company as a serious competitor in the automotive world and laid the foundation for BMW’s future as a manufacturer of sporty, high-quality, and premium vehicles.
